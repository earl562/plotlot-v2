# Comparable Sales Methodology for Land

## What Is a Comp?
A comparable sale (comp) is a recently sold property similar to the subject in location, zoning, and size, used to estimate the subject's market value.

## Comp Selection Criteria
1. **Proximity:** Within 3 miles of subject (closer = better)
2. **Recency:** Sold within last 12 months (6 months preferred)
3. **Zoning similarity:** Same or equivalent zoning classification
4. **Size similarity:** Lot size within +/-30% of subject
5. **Arm's length:** Exclude $0 transfers, foreclosures, family sales

## Adjustment Factors
- **Time:** Adjust for market appreciation (typically 3-8% annually)
- **Size:** Larger lots have lower $/acre (diminishing returns)
- **Location:** Adjust for proximity to amenities, roads, flood zones
- **Zoning:** Higher density zoning commands premium
- **Utilities:** Availability of water, sewer, electric

## Key Metrics
- **Price per acre** = Sale price / acreage
- **Price per buildable unit** = Sale price / max allowable units
- **ADV (After Development Value)** = What completed units sell for
- **Residual land value** = GDV - costs - margin

## Confidence Scoring
| Comp Count | Recency | Confidence |
|-----------|---------|------------|
| 5+ comps | < 6 months | 0.9-1.0 |
| 3-4 comps | < 12 months | 0.7-0.8 |
| 1-2 comps | < 12 months | 0.4-0.6 |
| 0 comps | — | 0.0 |

## Pro Forma (Residual Land Valuation)
```
Gross Development Value = Max Units x ADV per Unit
- Hard Costs = Max Units x Construction $/sf x Avg SF/Unit
- Soft Costs = Hard Costs x 20%
- Builder Margin = GDV x 25-30%
= Maximum Land Offer Price
```

## South Florida Market Defaults
- Construction cost: $175-225/sf (multifamily), $150-200/sf (single family)
- Average unit size: 850-1,200 sf (multifamily), 1,500-2,500 sf (single family)
- Builder margin: 25-30% of GDV
- Soft costs: 18-22% of hard costs
